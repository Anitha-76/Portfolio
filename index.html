About

Research-Informed.
Practice-Driven.

I am a learning designer and researcher currently completing my Ed.M. at Harvard Graduate School of Education (LDIT), where I design instructional tools, evaluate learning programs, and explore how AI can support — rather than replace — deep human learning.

Before Harvard, I spent over a decade working across government education systems, higher education, and nonprofits in India — including leading a statewide training program for 15,000 school leaders across Tamil Nadu in partnership with the State Education Department.

My work sits at the intersection of learning science, instructional design, and educational technology. I am drawn to problems where research meets real-world complexity — where the messy realities of classrooms, institutions, and learners demand more than theory alone.

I hold a PhD in Information Science & Technology from Anna University and have published six peer-reviewed articles. My research focus has evolved from computational systems to the human systems of learning and organizational change.
