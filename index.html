About

Research-informed.
Practice-driven.

I am a learning designer and researcher currently completing my Ed.M. at Harvard Graduate School of Education (Learning Design, Innovation & Technology), where I design instructional tools, evaluate learning programs, and explore how AI can support — rather than replace — deep human learning.

Before Harvard, I spent over a decade working across government education systems, higher education, and nonprofits in India — including leading a statewide training program for school leaders in Tamil Nadu in partnership with the State Education Department, and managing national-scale content development at IIT Madras.

My work sits at the intersection of learning science, instructional design, and educational technology. I hold a PhD in Information Science & Technology from Anna University and have published six peer-reviewed articles. My research has evolved from computational systems to the human systems of learning and organizational change.

Learning Design · HPL101
How People Learn: After-School Math Design Proposal
Harvard GSE · Fall 2025
Learning design proposal for an after-school online mathematics program serving middle school students from marginalized communities in Tamil Nadu government schools. Grounded in learning science frameworks including situated learning, constructivism, metacognition, and social-emotional learning, this design addresses foundational mathematics gaps documented in ASER and State Achievement Survey data. The program integrates real-world problem-solving, peer collaboration, reflective journaling, and emotional check-ins through a low-infrastructure, mobile-accessible format, designed specifically for first-generation learners in bilingual, low-resource contexts.

Systems Change · DEV320M
Scaling & Systems Change Analysis: eVidyaloka
Harvard GSE · Fall 2025
Scaling analysis of eVidyaloka, a nonprofit bridging the teacher shortage crisis in India's rural government schools through volunteerism and technology. Applying the DEV320M scaling framework, including causal mapping, systems diagnosis, theory of change, and pathway analysis, this paper evaluates eVidyaloka's strategy for reaching 1 crore children across India's aspirational districts, examining the organizational, systemic, and financing conditions required to move from 2 lakh students today to sustainable impact at national scale.

Policy Analysis · A801
Comparative Education Policy Analysis
Harvard GSE · Fall 2025
Policy analysis examining why teacher intrinsic motivation remains persistently low in Tamil Nadu government schools despite significant public investment and what can realistically be done about it. Drawing on Self-Determination Theory, international comparative evidence, and ground-level data from Tamil Nadu's 37,000 schools, this analysis identifies four interconnected root causes, evaluates four policy alternatives across equity, feasibility, scalability and flexibility criteria, and recommends a phased implementation strategy designed for a fiscally constrained public system.

Organizational Strategy · A012
Why Innovations in Education Fail
Harvard GSE · Fall 2025
Strategic analysis examining whether Sarvam AI, India's state-backed sovereign large language model, can meaningfully transform teaching and learning in vernacular K-12 classrooms. Applying Christensen's disruptive innovation theory, interdependence-modularity framework, value network analysis, and discovery-driven planning, this paper argues that Sarvam's multilingual, voice-first design positions it as education-enabling infrastructure for the 200 million+ students learning in regional languages, a market global LLMs are structurally disincentivized to serve.
